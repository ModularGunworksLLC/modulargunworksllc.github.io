# Alabama & Huntsville: Law Summary for Firearms Retailers

**For Modular Gunworks LLC, Huntsville, AL.**  
This is a summary for your reference only, not legal advice. Verify with an Alabama attorney or the ATF/state authorities.

---

## Federal (applies everywhere, including Alabama)

- **FFL required** to sell firearms as a business. You have one.
- **Age at sale (FFL):**
  - **Long guns (rifles, shotguns) and long-gun ammo:** 18+
  - **Handguns, frames/receivers, handgun ammo:** 21+
- **Transfers:** Firearms must transfer through an FFL; background check (NICS or state equivalent) at transfer.
- **Ammunition:** No federal license required to sell ammo; cannot sell to someone you have “reason to believe” is under 18 (long gun) or under 21 (handgun).

---

## Alabama state law

### Age

- **Pistols:** State law prohibits selling, giving, or lending **pistols** to anyone under **18**. Possession of pistols by under-18 is also restricted (exceptions for supervised use, etc.).
- **Federal applies to FFLs:** For a licensed dealer, federal age rules apply: 18 for long guns/long-gun ammo, 21 for handguns/handgun ammo. When state and federal differ, the **higher** age applies. So for you as an FFL: **21 for handguns and handgun ammo, 18 for long guns and long-gun ammo.**

### Handgun dealer license (state + local)

- **Retail dealers of handguns** (not just rifles/shotguns) must have a **state handgun dealer license** and a **local license** from the city/town where the business operates.
- **Local license:** Issued by the city (e.g., Huntsville) for the location where you operate. Valid **no more than one year**; must be **displayed** on premises where easily readable.
- **Conditions:** Business may operate only in the building designated on the license. A small fee (e.g., $0.50) goes to the State Treasury.
- **Rifles/shotguns only:** If you only sold long guns, the state handgun-dealer license wouldn’t apply; because you sell handguns (or handgun-related), you need the state + local handgun dealer license.

### Ammunition

- Alabama defines ammunition broadly (fixed cartridges, shotgun shells, components, projectiles, propellants). There is **no separate state license** just for selling ammunition. Federal age rules (18/21) apply.

### State preemption

- Alabama law gives the **state** “complete control” over regulation of firearms, ammunition, and firearm accessories so that rules are uniform. Cities and towns generally **cannot** adopt their own firearm/ammo rules that conflict. So **Huntsville cannot add extra age or “no-sale” rules** that go beyond state/federal.

### Record keeping

- A 2015 state law (H.B. 47) removed certain **state** mandatory record-keeping for handgun sales. **Federal** record-keeping (e.g., Form 4473, A&D book) still applies to your FFL.

---

## Huntsville (local)

- **Preemption:** Huntsville cannot impose its own firearm/ammunition retail rules beyond what state/federal allow (e.g., no local age or permit-to-purchase for customers).
- **Handgun dealer license:** If your business is in Huntsville city limits, you need the **local handgun dealer license** from the City of Huntsville (renewed annually, displayed on premises).
- **Zoning / business rules:** General zoning and business ordinances apply (e.g., where a gun store can operate), but they cannot single out or restrict lawful firearm sales beyond that.
- **Specific ordinances:** For exact wording (e.g., license application, display), check the [City of Huntsville Codes and Ordinances](https://www.huntsvilleal.gov/box-codes-and-ordinances/) or contact the City Clerk (e.g., 256-427-5427).

Age verification for the actual **transfer** happens at the FFL; for ammo, self-certification at checkout is consistent with the federal “reason to believe” standard and common industry practice.
