FFL Transfer Requirements

All firearms purchased from Modular Gunworks LLC must be transferred through a licensed Federal Firearms Dealer (FFL) in your state of residence. We do not ship firearms directly to individuals.

  1. Selecting Your FFL: You must arrange in advance for a licensed FFL in your state to receive your firearm. We can provide an FFL locator tool.
  2. Verification: You must provide the FFL's license information before we ship. Firearms will ONLY be shipped to the address listed on the FFL's federal license.
  3. Transfer Responsibility: You are responsible for all FFL transfer fees charged by your dealer. Modular Gunworks LLC is not responsible for these costs.
  4. Failed Background Check: If you fail a background check, the firearm will be returned to us at your expense. No refund will be issued for NICS delays or failures.
  5. Return Shipping: If an FFL refuses the transfer for any reason, you are responsible for return shipping costs ($40+ depending on carrier).

Background Checks (NICS - Brady Act)

All firearm purchases are subject to a background check conducted by the National Instant Criminal Background Check System (NICS) administered by the FBI. You have the right to challenge the results of your background check. For appeals or questions, contact the FBI directly at 1-877-324-6427.

Background Check Timeline:

  • Instant approval - same day (most common)
  • Delayed - up to 3 business days for FBI decision
  • Denied - you have the right to appeal (see appeal instructions from your FFL)

Form 4473

When you complete your firearm transfer with an FFL, you will be required to complete ATF Form 4473 (Firearms Transaction Record). This form requires you to certify your identity, age, state of residence, and compliance with federal law. Providing false information on Form 4473 is a federal crime.
